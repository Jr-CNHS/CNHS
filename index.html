GAS

The General Academic Strand (GAS) is a senior high school track that provides students with a comprehensive and well-rounded education. It is designed to equip students with the necessary knowledge and skills to pursue various fields of study in higher education. The GAS curriculum includes subjects such as Humanities, Social Sciences, Natural Sciences, and Mathematics, offering a broad foundation for students to explore different disciplines. This strand is suitable for students who are interested in pursuing college degrees in fields such as liberal arts, social sciences, business, and other non-specialized courses. Students who choose the General Academic Strand are prepared for a wide range of career opportunities and further academic pursuits after completing senior high school.

TVL AGRI

The Technical-Vocational-Livelihood (TVL) track with a specialization in Agriculture (AGRI) is a senior high school program that focuses on providing students with knowledge and skills related to agricultural practices and entrepreneurship. This track is designed to prepare students for careers in the agriculture industry, equipping them with practical skills that are essential for agricultural production, management, and sustainability.

Students enrolled in the TVL-AGRI track typically study subjects such as crop production, animal husbandry, agricultural technology, farm management, and agricultural entrepreneurship. Through hands-on training and experiential learning, students in this track develop competencies in farming techniques, livestock management, crop cultivation, and agricultural business operations.

TVL ICT

The Information and Communications Technology (ICT) track is a senior high school program that focuses on providing students with knowledge and skills related to technology, computer systems, programming, and digital communication. This track is designed to prepare students for careers in the rapidly evolving field of information technology and digital innovation.

Students enrolled in the ICT track typically study subjects such as computer programming, web development, multimedia arts, network systems, and information and communication technology. The curriculum emphasizes both theoretical knowledge and practical skills in areas such as software development, database management, digital media production, and IT project management.
